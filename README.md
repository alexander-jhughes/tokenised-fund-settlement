# Tokenised Fund Settlement
Cross-border fund settlement today creates counterparty risk, operational risk, and added cost, driven by AML/KYC checks, correspondent banking networks, and standard T+2 settlement cycles. Sovereign wealth funds in markets such as Singapore and the UAE rely on efficient cross-border capital movement, and this friction can slow or complicate their ability to allocate into tokenised fund products. Increasing straight-through processing through tokenised and stablecoin-based settlement systems can reduce these frictions by cutting out intermediary banks and lowering settlement risk. This project explores how such a system could work, grounded in ongoing infrastructure work such as Singapore's Project Guardian.
